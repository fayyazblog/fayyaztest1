A BIOGRAPHICAL SKETCH OF SARDAR DYAL SINGH MAJITHIA

Sardar Dyal Singh Majithia was born in 1849 in Kashi (India). His father, Sardar Lehna Singh, belonged to an eminent and respectable Sikh family of Shergill Jat Tribe. He served Maharaja Ranjit Singh , the prominent Sikh Ruler of the Punjab, and was granted vast Jagirs for his loyalty and his scholarly pursuits, in which he had few equals in his life-time. In 1832, Sardar Lehna Singh was appointed the Governor of Amritsar and he became quite popular as an Administrator, too. Maharaja Ranjit Singh conferred the title of “Husn-al-Dawlah” on him. Owing to the Court intrigues, he shifted to Banaras and died in Kashi 1854.

After the death of his father, Sardar Dyal Singh Majithia who was then only six years old and the only offspring of his father, migrate to village Majithia, where most of the Jagir of his father was situated. The vast Jagir was placed under the Court of Ward, owing to the infancy of Sardar Dyal Singh, under the custody of another Sikh Sardar, Taj Singh, and was transferred to him in 1870 according to the Will of his father. Sardar Dyal Singh attended the Mission Church School at Amritsar. He married Bhagwan Kaur, the daughter of Raja Sher Singh of Ambala. He had no issues.

Sradar Dyal Singh later shifted to Lahore and soon made his mark with poets, sportsmen, musicians, etc., in which Lahore of the 19th century abounded. In 1876, he left for England for higher studies and returned to Lahore after two years and established himself at the Empress Road. In Lahore, he associated himself with the Brahmo Samaj Movement, founded by Raja Ram Mohan Roy.

Sardar Dyal Singh had the company of illustrious friends such as Sir Pratul Chandra Chatterjee, a Rais of Lahore and a senior lawyer; Chandra Nath Bose, Registrar of the Punjab University; Navin Chandra Roy the founder of Brahmo Samaj in the Punjab, Raj Bahadur Mul Raj, the first Punjabi to get an M.A. degree of the Calcutta University, Pandit Sivanath Shastri, a leader of the Brahmo Samaj, etc. Sardar Dyal Singh was a man of literary pursuits. He composed verses and wrote a number of tracts on philosophical and general topics. He translated the dialogues of Raja Ram Mohan Roy, which was warmly received by the literary circles. He gave extensive financial assistance to the Brahmo Samaj Movement and helped other charitable institutions.
In 188, he started the weekly “Tribune”, which became a daily after his death. In 1882 ,an “Indian Association” was setup in Lahore and Sardar Dyal Singh was elected its first President. He was among the first ten members of the Punjab Public Library.
He was an active figure of the Indian National Congress and attended its Allahabad Session in 1888. He was a great philanthropist and supported all good causes. Though he physically expired on 9th September 1898, as a result of severe illness, yet he lives on through his generous legacy, which enabled the establishment of the Sardar Dyal Singh Trust Library and the Dyal Singh Collage in Lahore.

He fell ill on 25th August, 1898 and expired on 9th September, 1898.
